2025 USA 80+ HOCKEY HALL OF FAME INDUCTEE

CHARLES M. SCHULZ (1922–2000)
Ashland, Wisconsin → Santa Rosa, California

A LIFE ON ICE – FROM BACKYARD RINKS TO A WORLD STAGE
Best known as the creator of *Peanuts*, Charles M. “Sparky” Schulz also nurtured a lifelong love for hockey. Growing up in Minnesota, he learned to skate on backyard rinks and frozen ponds, often using makeshift equipment and even enlisting his grandmother as a basement goalie. After moving to California, Schulz built the Redwood Empire Ice Arena in 1969, creating a new hub for hockey and skating in Santa Rosa.

  • Founder, Redwood Empire Ice Arena (1969)
  • Creator of the Snoopy Senior World Hockey Tournament (est. 1975)
  • Played weekly until 1999, just before his passing
  • Over 150 *Peanuts* comic strips featured hockey

Legacy: Schulz used his art to celebrate and promote the game, turning beloved characters like Snoopy and Peppermint Patty into hockey icons. His Snoopy Tournament drew NHL alumni and international teams, helping to elevate senior hockey on a global scale.

Honors: Inducted into the U.S. Hockey Hall of Fame (1993) and recipient of the Lester Patrick Award (1981) for service to hockey in the U.S.

Congratulations to Charles M. Schulz — a skater, a storyteller, and a cultural ambassador whose love for the game continues to inspire.

From pond hockey to high school competition to varsity play at Tufts University, Vic’s passion for hockey never waned. Now based in Califon, New Jersey, his lifelong love of the sport continues, even into his 80s.

  • Invited to the 1960 USA Olympic Team Tryouts
  • Defense — wore jersey #14
  • Plays weekly with the 50+ Huff’s and Puff’s group
  • USA Hockey Referee in ECAC — "you skate 100% of the game"

Why He Plays: “I am an area USA Hockey Referee in ECAC. As a referee, you skate 100% of the game.”

What He Loves Most: The camaraderie. The skating. The thrill of international games. For Vic, hockey is more than a game — it’s a global brotherhood.

Congratulations to Vic Godleski — a player, coach, referee, and gentleman whose skates still carve the ice with purpose.
